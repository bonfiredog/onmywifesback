In the winter of 1852, Isaak Charles Walter Scinbank (1808-1878) left his home in Derbyshire, England to set sail from London aboard his ship, the H.P.S. Otranto. His destination was the Canadian Arctic, to search for and rescue the decorated explorer Sir John Franklin. Franklin had gone missing seven years previously whilst attempting to complete the Northwest Passage, a hypothetical route west through the thick offshore ice and south into the Bering Sea.

Against the express wishes of his sponsor, Franklin's wife Lady Jane, Scinbank kept a detailed diary during his voyage. Thanks to the Scinbank Collection and the tireless digitisation efforts of the British Library, this manuscript is presented to the public for the first time.
